# Advice for Avoiding Spam Filters and Crafting Effective Phishing Emails

To ensure your phishing campaigns are successful, it's critical to craft emails that bypass spam filters and appear legitimate to victims. Below are key strategies and best practices for designing emails that avoid detection and build trust with your targets.

## Avoid Directly Copying Vendor Emails or Websites

When creating phishing emails, resist the temptation to copy content directly from vendor emails (e.g., Microsoft account alerts, Google account alerts). These templates are well-known to spam filters and are easily flagged. Additionally, copying vendor websites often triggers protections designed to prevent replication, making your phishing page ineffective.

Instead, use vendor emails and websites as inspiration. Study their structure, tone, and formatting, but build your content from the ground up. This approach not only helps evade spam filters but also allows you to customize the email to better suit your target.

## Leverage HTML Formatting for Realism

Many phishing tools support HTML editing, enabling you to create highly realistic emails. Use this capability to design emails that mirror the look and feel of legitimate communications. Custom HTML formatting allows you to:
- Match the branding and style of the organization or tool you're impersonating.
- Avoid the generic appearance of plain-text emails, which are more likely to raise suspicion.
- Build trust with victims by presenting a polished, professional email.

## Avoid Urgent or Trigger Words

Spam filters often flag emails containing urgent language (e.g., "Immediate action required," "Your account will be suspended"). While urgency can be an effective social engineering tactic, it must be used sparingly and subtly. Aim for a tone that conveys importance without triggering automated filters.

## Use Spellcheck and AI Tools

If English is not your first language, or even if it is, use spellcheck and AI tools to refine your email content. These tools can help you:
- Correct grammar and spelling errors.
- Improve sentence structure and flow.
- Ensure the language sounds natural and professional.

Well-written emails are less likely to raise suspicion and more likely to be trusted by victims.

## Customize Emails to the Client's Dialect and Tendencies

Regional dialects and cultural tendencies can significantly impact how an email is perceived. Research the organization's location and the regional language nuances of your target audience. For example:
- In the United States, phrases and word choices can vary widely between regions (e.g., the South vs. the Northeast).
- For international targets, consider local idioms, formalities, and communication styles.

By tailoring your email to match these nuances, you build familiarity and increase the likelihood of success.

## Leverage OSINT for Targeted Customization

The quality of your phishing campaign depends on the quality of your OSINT (open-source intelligence). Gather as much information as possible about:
- **The Organization:** Understand its structure, industry, and common communication styles.
- **The Victims:** Research individual targets to identify personal or professional details that can be used to personalize emails.
- **Common IT Tools:** Identify tools used by the organization (e.g., Salesforce, Workday) and study their email templates.

### Example: Customizing Based on IT Tools
If your OSINT reveals that a company uses Workday for HR processes:
1. Search for "Workday email templates" online to see examples of legitimate Workday emails.
2. Note the formatting, tone, and common phrases used in these emails.
3. Build a custom HTML email that mimics the Workday style but is tailored to your phishing objective.

This level of detail makes your email appear authentic and increases the chances that victims will engage with it.

## Test and Iterate

Before launching your campaign, test your emails thoroughly. Send them to your client point of contact and verify:
- The email bypasses spam filters and lands in the inbox.
- The formatting renders correctly across different devices and email clients.
- Any links or calls-to-action function as intended.

By refining your emails based on testing, you can ensure they are both undetectable and effective.

## Conclusion

Crafting phishing emails that evade spam filters and build victim trust requires a combination of technical skill, creativity, and research. By avoiding direct copies of vendor content, leveraging HTML formatting, customizing for regional dialects, and using OSINT to inform your designs, you can create highly effective campaigns. Remember, the success of your phishing engagement hinges on the quality of your emails—invest the time to make them as legitimate and targeted as possible.
